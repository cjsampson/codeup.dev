Washington State, California, and Florida BEST Amusement Parks

Washington State Fair

  • The 1917 Merry-Go-Round is the oldest active carnival ride at the Fair, operating here since 1923.
  • 84,847 riders were not ashamed to "scream like a kid" on the Classic Coaster at the 2015 Washington State Fair.
  • The Skyride debuted at the Fair in 1980, carrying riders 55 feet in the air.
  • 28,352 people braved the Rainier Rush roller coaster at the 2015 Washington State Fair.

Six Flags Magic Mountain

  • In the 1971 season, Magic Mountain obtained permission from Warner Bros. to use the Looney Tunes characters.
  • With the opening of Great American Revolution in 1976, Magic Mountain became the first park in the world to have a modern, 360-degree steel looping coaster (though previous roller coasters with loops had been built and dismantled elsewhere due to safety issues).
  • In 1979 the park was sold to Six Flags and became known as Six Flags Magic Mountain in 1980.
  • In 1988 Ninja, "The Black Belt of Roller Coasters", opened. Built by Arrow Dynamics, it was the first suspended swing roller coaster on the West coast.

Magic Kingdom

  • The Winnie the Pooh attraction in Fantasyland is housed in the site where Mr. Toad’s Wild Ride existed until 1998.
  • The now extinct Mickey’s Toontown Fair was originally known as Birthdayland and then Mickey’s Starland.
  • Splash Mountain in Adventureland has a five-story plunge at a 45-degree angle at a speed of 40 miles per hour.
  • The names of the 3 talking heads who introduce the Country Bear Jamboree are Buff, max, and Melvin.
